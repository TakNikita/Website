Altie Reality

Virtual, Augmented and Mixed Reality solutions for Healthcare

Extended Reality is redefining the complexity and understanding of human anatomy and diagnostics in a way that seems straight out of science fiction. The complexity of human anatomy, sensitivity of the data and timely diagnosis has been the biggest challenge for medical fraternity since the dawn of modern medical science. XR takes away the subjectivity of human spatial understanding and provides a uniform and coherent way to understand the anatomy in a more intuitive way. Using XR, the medical students can now get a multidimensional understanding of a patients anatomy, surgeons can simulate complex surgeries without risks, diagnostics can overlay scan and x-ray data on the patient to understand to have a more contextual understanding and patient history can be presented in an operation theater without any peripherals. AR based Remote assist and diagnostics can help save lives in case of unavailability of trained medics in case of emergency or calamity and virtual surgeries can be performed to help train the future live savers.

MR use cases

  • AR based human models can assist in understanding of human anatomy in medical students

  • Overlay of MRI scan on patients using AR for in context diagnostics and surgical aid

  • Enhanced visualization of internal organs for treatment planning

  • AR based human models can be used for virtual surgeries for training and preoperative planning

  • Remote assist in case of natural disasters and calamities

Benefits of MR in Medical

Up to 250% improvement in ability to complete a medical procedure with AR training

Up to 80% better understanding of human anatomy and visualization in AR trained medical students

Up to 400% increased retention of VR based training in medical students

VR trained surgeons are likely to make 6X less mistakes

VR trained surgeons are up to 30% faster

Up to 150% better attention to training and 3.5X better emotional connection to the content

AR based preoperative training can improve the success of a surgery by up to 200%
