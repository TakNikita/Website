AR, VR and MR Development Company for Industrial Machinery

Industrial Machinery probably has the highest possibilities for adoption and application of immersive technologies given the vast variety of equipment, industries and technologies this industry segment caters to. The high level of dependency on experts to carry out installation, critical troubleshooting and maintenance procedures has been a bottleneck in growth of industrial equipment manufacturers. Coupled with uptime commitments and churn of trained operators/maintenance technicians at client end has been an Achilles heel for the industry. Using mixed reality, not only now we can leverage the available time of the experts to it’s maximum potential, but can have dramatic improvements on equipment uptime, training of new operators and even hazardous equipment operator trainings. MR can also play a key role in marketing of equipment, as digital versions can be presented as expos and conferences with no added logistic complexities and costs.

MR use cases

  • Installation / commissioning instructions

  • Layout planning and interface validation

  • Guided Operator instructions

  • Remote Assist / Troubleshooting

  • Safety Trainings

  • Realtime / contextual IoT data monitoring

  • Spare Part identification and 3D BoM

  • Maintenance instructions and records

  • Virtual Demonstration and marketing

Benefits of MR in Industrial-Machinery

MR can assist maintenance technicians in diagnosing and repairing industrial machinery. Technicians can use MR devices to overlay digital information, such as schematics and step-by-step instructions, onto physical equipment, guiding them through complex repair procedures.

MR is employed for training industrial machinery operators and technicians. Trainees can engage in realistic simulations of machinery operation and maintenance tasks in a virtual environment, providing a safe and controlled learning experience.

MR technology aids in quality control processes by overlaying digital information on physical products during inspection. This helps operators identify defects, deviations, or assembly errors, ensuring that products meet quality standards.
